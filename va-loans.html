Loan Program

VA Loans

VA-backed home loans help eligible Veterans, service members, and surviving spouses buy, build, improve, or refinance a home through participating lenders.

Start The Intake Back To Loan Programs

How This Loan Works

With a VA-backed loan, the Department of Veterans Affairs guarantees a portion of the loan made by a private lender. That guaranty helps reduce lender risk and can allow qualified borrowers to receive favorable terms.

VA explains that many VA-backed loans are made with no down payment. Borrowers still need to meet credit, income, occupancy, property, entitlement, and lender requirements, and eligibility is generally documented through a Certificate of Eligibility.

Why Borrowers Choose It

VA financing can be powerful for eligible borrowers because it may reduce upfront cash needs and can offer competitive terms compared with traditional private mortgage options.

Important Tradeoffs

VA loans may include a VA funding fee unless the borrower qualifies for an exemption. Eligibility, entitlement, property use, and lender overlays should all be reviewed before assuming the program fits.

Who It May Fit

VA loans may fit eligible borrowers who want to buy or refinance a primary residence and preserve cash at closing. They can also be useful for borrowers comparing payment, cash-to-close, and long-term cost across FHA, conventional, and jumbo alternatives.
