Bank Statement Loans

Bank statement loans are alternative documentation mortgage options for self-employed borrowers whose tax returns may not show the full cash flow available to support a mortgage payment.

Start The Intake Back To Loan Programs

How This Loan Works

Instead of using only W-2s, pay stubs, or tax-return income, a bank statement loan may review deposits from personal or business bank statements over a set period. Many programs look at 12 or 24 months of statements, then calculate qualifying income from average eligible deposits after applying any required expense factor.

The review still considers the full mortgage picture. Credit history, down payment, reserves, property type, occupancy, debt obligations, and loan-to-value can all affect whether a borrower fits a specific program.

Why Self-Employed Borrowers Use It

Business owners, contractors, consultants, real estate professionals, and other self-employed borrowers may have legitimate cash flow that is not fully reflected by taxable income after deductions. Bank statement documentation can help tell a more complete income story.

Important Tradeoffs

Bank statement loans are commonly treated as non-QM or alternative documentation products, so rates, fees, down payment requirements, and reserves may differ from conventional mortgage programs. Requirements vary by lender and should be reviewed before making an offer or refinance decision.

What Lenders Review

A lender may review recurring deposits, unusual large deposits, business expense assumptions, account ownership, credit profile, available assets, and whether the property and occupancy fit the program. Clean, organized bank statements can make the review easier and help avoid delays.

Who It May Fit

This loan may fit self-employed borrowers with stable bank deposits, strong payment history, and enough assets for down payment, closing costs, and reserves. It may be less useful when deposits are inconsistent, business expenses are difficult to document, or a conventional loan produces a better overall result.
